Recessions

Economies have periods of growth and contraction.

The times of contraction are referred to as recessions.

Four economic measurments have value in determining when the economy has entered a recession.

Unemployment

As an economy contracts the unemployment tends to rise.

The Shiny application includes a data series that plots the unemployement rate (U-3) alongside recessions

These recessions are defined by the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER).

Industrial Production

As an economy contracts it produces less, both of services and products.

One way to track the output of the economy is by measuring the output of the industrial sector.

The Shiny application includes a data series that plots industrial production against the NBER recessions.

Real retail sales

Contraction in spending usually accompanies a recession.

In recent decades in the U.S. consumer spending has been a key driver of the economy.

The Shiny application includes a data series of real retail sales (sales that have been corrected for inflation) against the NBER recession data.

Real personal income

Along with declinces in employment and production, incomes also decline during a recession.

The Bureau of Economic Analysis tracks real personal income (income corrected for inflation).

The Shiny application plots this real personal income against the NBER recession data.
